# Case study: AI sales research workspace

**Goal**: Give sales reps a single workspace where AI aggregates company research, finds contacts, and drafts personalized outreachâ€”cutting hours of prep into minutes.

## The challenge

Sales reps spend 30-40% of their time researching prospects. They bounce between LinkedIn, company websites, news articles, and CRM. By the time they're ready to call, they've forgotten half of what they read.

The workspace needs to aggregate, synthesize, and make research actionable.

## Interaction patterns

### Progressive loading
Don't block the whole page:

```
1. Instant: Show search, company name
2. Fast (1s): Basic profile, contacts
3. Background (2-5s): News, insights, AI drafts
```

Skeleton loaders for each section independently.

### Source verification
Every insight links to original source:

```html
<p>Acme launched a new API product last week.
   <a href="#" class="source-link">
     <span class="icon">ğŸ”—</span> Company Blog
   </a>
</p>
```

Sales reps need to verify before mentioning in calls.

### CRM sync
Two-way sync with Salesforce/HubSpot:

```html
<div class="crm-status">
  <span class="synced">âœ“ Synced to Salesforce</span>
  <button>View in CRM</button>

```

Data entered here should appear in CRM. Data from CRM should appear here.

## Metrics that matter

- **Prep time reduction**: Hours saved per account
- **Insight usage**: Do reps use AI suggestions in calls?
- **Outreach conversion**: Do AI-drafted messages perform better?
- **Data freshness**: How often is stale data flagged?

## What good looks like

1. Rep types "Acme Corp"
2. Within 1 second: company card, basic profile
3. Within 3 seconds: contacts, news, AI insights
4. Rep sees: "Series B last month" flagged as high priority
5. One click: "Use in outreach" pre-fills email draft
6. Rep edits 2 sentences, clicks "Copy & Send"
7. Auto-logged to Salesforce with all context

Total time: 3 minutes vs. 30 minutes manual research. And the outreach is more personalized because the insights were surfaced, not buried in 15 browser tabs.
